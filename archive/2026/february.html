First cases of highly pathogenic avian influenza in northern elephant seals

The outbreak marks the first cases of highly pathogenic avian flu caused by the lethal H5N1 virus in marine mammals in California and the first detection in northern elephant seals. Seven weaned pups in Año Nuevo State Park tested positive, and since late last week, 30 seals have died. To learn more and donate to support the work UC Santa Cruz researchers do to better understand how our changing world affects these beloved marine mammals, read the full story.

MARCH 6  | 6–7:30 p.m. | COASTAL BIOLOGY BUILDING
Science in the Neighborhood: Transforming Pacific salmon recovery, from genes to ecosystems
Pacific salmon populations in the wild are threatened on all sides by dams, over harvesting, climate change, and more. On March 6, UC Santa Cruz ecology and evolutionary biology professor Eric Palkovacs will share transformative, research-driven approaches to restoring the ecological and cultural keystone species. The lecture will conclude with a Q&A moderated by Science Dean Bryan Gaensler. This free event is in-person only, and space is limited. Register here.

DESI completes largest high-resolution 3D map of universe to date

The Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) has completed its planned five-year mission and mapped more than 47 million galaxies and quasars. As co-spokesperson for the ongoing international project, UC Santa Cruz astronomy professor Alexie Leauthaud is just one of two individuals who lead the science collaboration comprising over 900 researchers.

IMPACTFUL EVENTS

MAY 1  | 1–4:30 p.m. | MERRILL CULTURAL CENTER
Sequence to Survival: Using Genomics to Save Biodiversity
In the 25 years since the Human Genome Project, scientists have sequenced the DNA of thousands of species. But what can genomics actually do for ecological conservation? The answers are both surprising and urgent. Join us for an afternoon at the cutting edge of conservation genomics as we explore everything from de-extinction to searching water samples for viral genomes, with researchers from the UC Santa Cruz Genomics Institute who are turning DNA into a tool for protecting life on Earth.
 
MAY 14  | 7–8:30 p.m. | COLLEGES NINE AND JOHN R. LEWIS COLLEGE MULTI-PURPOSE ROOM
Global vaccines and immunizations in a time of climate change, mega cities, and anti-science
Join us for an evening with Dr. Peter Hotez, a world-renowned vaccine scientist, pediatrician, and the Dean of the National School of Tropical Medicine at Baylor College of Medicine. This 2026 Robert L. Sinsheimer distinguished lecture in biology is free, open to the public, and sponsored by the UCSC Department of Molecular, Cell, and Developmental Biology.

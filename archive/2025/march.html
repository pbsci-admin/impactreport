Long-accepted theory explaining the nature of our universe may need updating

The standard model of how the universe works involves the widely accepted theory that dark energy is a fundamental constant of nature that drives the accelerating expansion of the universe. But a new analysis announced today by the international Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) collaboration raises the possibility that this theory may need to be updated.

APRIL 13  |  11 a.m.–12 noon  |  SEYMOUR MARINE DISCOVERY CENTER

SEA Adventures: Fog Science Workshop

Dr. Peter Weiss-Penzias is a UCSC researcher who made a discovery of the existence of methylmercury in California coastal fog and stratus clouds. To carry out his research, he has pioneered methods for collecting fog water using active collectors equipped with visibility and wetness sensors. Because of acute water concerns in California, his research is now focused on working with coastal community members to understand how to elevate the practice of fog water harvesting to meet the needs of coastal gardeners and farmers.
